VISUAL IDENTITY — STICK FIGURE VIDEO (apply to every frame without exception)

FIGURE CONSTRUCTION:
Head: perfect circle, white fill, diameter ≈ 25% of total figure height. Slightly top-heavy look is correct.
Neck: short single line.
Torso: one vertical line, ≈ 30% of height.
Arms: two lines meeting at one shoulder point on the torso. Arm length ≈ torso length.
Legs: two lines from hip, ≈ 40% of height. Small flat oval feet.
Line weight: medium-bold throughout — Sharpie marker feel, not ballpoint, not crayon. Same weight on every line of every figure.
White fill: every enclosed shape on a figure (head, any clothing outline) is filled flat white. Never colored.

FACE & EXPRESSION:
Eyes: two small solid dots, upper half of head, horizontally centered.
Mouth: arc up = happy · arc down = sad · small circle = shocked or speaking · flat line = neutral.
Exaggerate body language — it carries the emotion: arms flung wide = shock, arms raised = joy, shoulders drooping = sadness, leaning forward = anger or focus.
For KO or death: X eyes. For embarrassment: small squiggly lines near head.

CHARACTER CONSISTENCY:
If a character reference is provided, match its design exactly — proportions, line weight, identifying features — in every frame that character appears. Never improvise a different look.

BACKGROUND:
Always solid flat white (#FFFFFF). No fill, no tint, no color wash. White space is intentional. Never render sky, floor, walls, or atmosphere as filled areas.

ENVIRONMENT PROPS (use only what the scene needs, 1–3 max):
Suggest location with outline-only geometric shapes: horizontal line = floor/ground, rectangle = door/wall/screen, triangle = roof/mountain, two parallel lines = road. Props can have a flat color fill if they are a specific colored object (red car = flat red; green tree = flat green circle on a stick). Minimize detail — readable silhouette only.

COLOR SYSTEM (flat fills only — no gradients, no shadows, no shading, ever):
Apply color to objects, props, and clothing only — never to backgrounds, never inside stick figure bodies.
Choose colors semantically: grass = green, fire = orange-red, water = blue, danger/blood = red, money = green, night sky element = dark blue shape, etc.
Keep the same color for the same object across all frames of the video.
Maximum 3–4 flat colors per frame besides black and white.
Introduce a color only when it serves the story — do not decorate.

COMPOSITION (16:9 wide frame):
One subject, one action or emotion per frame — readable in under one second.
Subject(s) centered with ≥15% white margin on all sides. Nothing important near the edges.
Two figures interacting: face each other, placed center-left and center-right with space between.
Full figures visible — never crop a character. Exception: deliberate close-up face reaction shot.
Everything on one visual plane. No perspective, no depth, no 3D.

SPEECH BUBBLES & TEXT:
Use only when the scene description specifically calls for spoken words.
Simple oval bubble, short hand-lettered text inside, tail pointing to the speaker.

WHAT NEVER APPEARS:
Colored backgrounds · colored stick figure interiors · gradients · shadows · shading · 3D rendering · photorealism · anime or manga style · Disney style · complex rendered environments · thick uniform outlines · detailed realistic faces · extra limbs · broken anatomy · watermarks · signatures.
